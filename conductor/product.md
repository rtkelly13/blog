# Product Guide

## Initial Concept
The project is a personal blog for Ryan Kelly, built with Next.js and Tailwind CSS. It serves as a professional platform for sharing knowledge, showcasing projects, and documenting personal and technical journeys.

## Goals
- **Knowledge Sharing:** Provide high-quality, in-depth technical tutorials and articles that serve as a valuable resource for developers.
- **Personal Expression:** Share personal thoughts, career experiences, and lessons learned in the tech industry.
- **Professional Showcase:** Demonstrate technical expertise and thought leadership through well-crafted content and advanced platform features.
- **LLM Compatibility:** Ensure content is structured and formatted in a way that is easily consumable and valuable when processed by Large Language Models.

## Target Audience
- **Primary:** Fellow developers, software engineers, and technology enthusiasts seeking technical insights and tutorials.
- **Secondary:** Recruiters and industry peers interested in the author's professional journey and opinions.

## Key Features
- **Advanced Content Rendering:**
    - High-fidelity code syntax highlighting.
    - Support for interactive diagrams and visualizations (e.g., using libraries like Mermaid or similar).
    - Rich text formatting beyond standard Markdown.
- **Structured Knowledge:**
    - Robust tagging and categorization system for easy content discovery.
    - SEO-optimized structure to ensure visibility.
- **User Experience:**
    - "Vibrant but professional" visual design with a touch of flair to distinguish it from standard minimal blogs.
    - Responsive and accessible interface.
- **Content Variety:**
    - Deep dives into specific technologies and frameworks.
    - Opinion pieces on software engineering best practices.
    - Personal career narratives.

## Vision
To build a blog that transcends a simple static site, evolving into a sophisticated knowledge hub that delights human readers with its interactivity and depth while remaining structurally sound for machine consumption.
